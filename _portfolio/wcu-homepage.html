Project showing you today is the redesign of the western carolina university homepage that launched last fall I worked on user experience, did both design and development on this project, and designed the new homepage from scratch I’m going to talk through the process of this project, and some of the tools used in the process

Project Goals

Goals accomplished in this redesign and development overhaul project:

  • Surface content marketing and news content
  • Surface important links for different audience groups
  • Reduce content repetition
  • Refresh the look and feel of the page to modernize the style and provide more breathing room while staying true to the university's brand
  • Add a promotional area for important non-story content
  • Provide a sense of familiarity in the updated design
  • Reduce load time of the page
  • Improve event representation and information

Process

User Experience Research

UX Input: External User Testing Internal Feedback Technical Issues

Prior to beginning the design process We conducted user testing with individuals in external prospective student and prospective parent demographics We also received feedback from within the department and institution, and we had some goals of our own. Some examples of feedback were….

  • Hidden story content
  • Unneeded descriptions
  • Repeated images
  • Broken if video or not square
  • Repeated events
  • Getting missed
  • Redundant content
  • Add Youtube and Twitter
  • Not able to curate

Early design

After gathering all of the data and feedback I began designing, starting with hand drawn sketches to quickly iterate through ideas

prototyping

Then I went into Adobe XD and Developed a handful of different versions showed them to a team of people in my department for feedback We selected one

refinement

I took the design we selected and worked out all of the details determined how the layout would respond, and added some prototyping interactivity to show how the page would function

Responsive Layouts

development and launch

For this project I managed and participated in the entire development process Developed the layout locally-- I use Brackets (which is Adobe’s source code editor) Part of the way through development another front-end developer joined the project and we collaborated on the code using Git then we integrated into the CMS in a test environment, which included building new elements into the site’s XSL After we worked out the bugs, we replaced the homepage with the new code, And launched the new page

Results

Improved UX New Content Areas Modernized Style

Results for improved user experience

  • Reduce load time of the page
  • Surface important links for different audience groups
  • Reduce content repetition
  • Cleaner interface
  • Improve event representation and information
  • While providing a sense of familiarity in the updated design

Other benefits of the new page include

  • An Added promotional area for important non-story content
  • Surfacing of content marketing and news content
  • And a Refreshed look and feel of the page to modernize the style and provide more breathing room while staying true to the university's brand
